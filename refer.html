The problem today

Referrals run on memory.
Accountability doesn't.

The informal referral economy in Indian healthcare moves thousands of patients every day — making them difficult to track transparently, acknowledge instantly, or audit systematically.

📋
Doctors have no referral record
Most referrals happen via phone or WhatsApp. Nothing is logged. Months later, there's no way to verify what was referred, to whom, or whether it was acted on.
📊
Hospitals & diagnostics run on Excel
Referral relationships are managed in spreadsheets that no one fully trusts. No operational insights, no visibility on referral activity insights, no accountability for who's been acknowledged.
🔕
No mutual acknowledgement loop
After a referral is made, neither party has a clean way to confirm it was received, acted on, or completed. Disputes fester with no paper trail on either side.
👥
Delegation exposes too much
When a hospital owner delegates tracking to a staff member, workflow visibility needs to be secure. Operations must be managed with role-based access to keep patient files private.
Why Mitr-AI Exists

Continuous records in patient workflows.

Healthcare referrals frequently occur through phone calls, messages, informal communication, and manual records. As referral volumes increase, maintaining visibility and continuity becomes challenging.

Mitr-AI helps healthcare providers maintain an organised, secure referral record and a mutual acknowledgement workflow, ensuring no patient gets lost in transition.
