The problem today

Referrals happen on trust.
Everything after that is a black box.

Once a doctor refers a patient and a hospital receives one, both sides are flying blind. No tracking, no feedback, no data.

📋
Doctors have no record
Most referrals happen via phone or WhatsApp and are never formally logged. If a patient outcome goes wrong, there's no documentation.
🔕
No feedback loop
After referring a patient, doctors rarely hear back from the hospital. They don't know if the patient was admitted, treated, or went elsewhere.
📊
Hospitals are guessing
Most hospitals don't know which doctors are referring the most patients, which specialties drive admissions, or why patients choose competitors.
🔗
No shared network
Doctors and hospitals operate as disconnected islands. There's no platform that connects the referring doctor to the receiving hospital in real time.
The opportunity

India's referral network is massive — and completely untracked.

Every day, hundreds of thousands of patients move between clinics and hospitals based on informal referrals. Mitrai brings intelligence to this invisible network.
